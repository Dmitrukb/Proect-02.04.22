Lutsk castle

Lutsk, Volyn region

Information

History

Lubart Castle as a whole is an equilateral triangle with protruding sides, in the tops there are towers, spaced about 100 m from each other. The height of the walls is about 12 m above the shaft. The thickness is up to 3 m, the upper part is 0.8 m thick. In total, up to 5 million bricks were used for the castle. During the six centuries of its existence, the floor level of the castle yard has risen by about 3.8 m. The walls and towers are built in the Gothic masonry system. The size of the bricks varies depending on the construction period in which they were used. In general, it is (28-30) x (12.5-15) x (7.5-9.5) cm. The walls contain battle galleries with loopholes . The walls are sometimes covered with a shingled roof. Bay windows are held on consoles near the Entrance and Bishop 'sTowersmachine guns - holes for shelling the area of ​​the sole of the wall.

The entrance tower is a five-story parallelepiped structure. Height is 28 m, size in plan 11.9 × 12.05 m. In the southern part there is a spiral staircase , and connected with the prince's palace. The wall of the Surrounding Castle starts from the corner of the tower. At the bottom, the tower is supported by powerful buttresses , erected at the beginning of the 19th century, which narrow slightly to the top. Between them is an arched entrance to the castle. Above the entrance are twoarched portals . These are the former entrances to the castle, once they were closed by a drawbridge. The second tier of the tower has semi-cylindrical, and the third and fourth had cruciform vaults. Only they break the generally symmetrical structure of the main facadetowers. In the middle part of the facade there are square teeth located above the intermediate cornice . The upper cornice has an arched motif and encircles the tower around the perimeter . Below it are two holes. Attic has powerful renaissance - shaped merlon teeth. The rear facade of the tower, which overlooks the castle courtyard, has a simpler structure. The central passage has a Gothic finish. The middle tiers of the tower, except for the upper one, have one window each, framed by white stone Renaissance platbands.

History

The Steering Tower , like the Entrance Tower, has the shape of a rectangular parallelepiped, supported on the outside by buttresses. Height 28 m, plan size 10 × 10 m. It also has two external branches: the eastern (shorter) is the remnant of the eastern defensive ring, the southern (longer) - the remnant of the Surrounding Castle. It has two entrances with white stone portals: the lower one was filled in for a long time, so the entrance of the second tier was used. On the walls you can see the ornamental type of brickwork. The five-tiered tower ends with an attic with teeth. In general, the thickness of the walls decreases upwards: on the lower tier it reaches 3 m, and on the upper (attic) - 75 cm. From the third tier there are exits to the battlefields of the walls. Loopholes are arranged in the walls of different floors, some of the windows are Gothicframing , others - Renaissance. The architecture of the Lord's Tower is even more concise. The height is 14 m, the size in the plan is 8.75 × 8.50 m. The thickness of the walls on the lower tier - up to 3 m, on the upper - about 1 m. The tower has no decoration. From the third tier there are exits to the battlefields of the walls. The vault above the second tier is cylindrical . Loop jumpers with arched finish. The tower is covered with a shingled tent roof, above which a metal figure "Clicker" rises on a leg . The tower of the Chortory princes of the Surrounding Castle is also a rectangular parallelepiped covered with a shingled pointed roof. Between the tower and the southern corner of the Jesuit College, the castle wall has loopholes. From the tower there is an exit to the battle gallery of this part of Okolnik. There is also an exit in the opposite direction - on the castle wall, which further extends to the south . Now this exit is partially walled up and left in the form of a window. The feature of the preserved parts of the Surrounding Castle is the foundation arches and ornamental masonry of some sections of the walls, where bricks of different colors were used.
